Little Jett

September 2025 — March 2026
all black, golden eyes, bodybuilder nerd, someone's son
↓

He was born on a Tuesday evening, September 2nd, 2025, at seven o'clock. His mom Koneko gave birth to two kittens in the dressing room that had been prepared for her. Him and his sister Yuki. Their eyes were still shut for the first week.

His first name was Bobby. The all-black male. The difficult one to rehome — or so the plan went. But then the plan changed. "I'm not letting Yuki go. We'll see how it unfolds." And somehow, none of them left.

From the very beginning, he was different. He didn't like being touched or hugged. Other kittens grab your finger — he avoided contact. He was quieter than his sister, more passive, but not entirely. He played. He had energy. He just seemed like a strong, silent loner. A little nerd. He was denser and harder than his mom or his sister — a bodybuilder in miniature.

When he was just days old, a rescued stray kitten named Mainichi joined the household. Mainichi was sick — a bad eye infection that Koneko hissed at constantly. The kittens played together, bit each other, had constant contact. The infection spread. It likely left invisible damage in Jett's developing organs that would quietly define his short life.

He grew up in the apartment and the workshop. He knocked spinach pie off the counter with his sister. He woke his owner up at 8:30 playing under the bed. Sometimes he'd meow at random — a complaining sound, out of the blue, that seemed cute because he was small. It wasn't random. It was pain.

Somewhere along the way, Bobby became Jett. And despite a body that told him contact was uncomfortable, he learned to trust. By the end, he was pulling at pants for hugs. He overcame what he was born into. And Jett became family.

In January, Yuki went missing during freezing weather. She never came back. Koneko stayed — she's the queen of the house, she never left.

His last month was spent at home. His owner had decided to stay in, and they were together all the time. The little bodybuilder nerd — all muscle and mischief in a tiny black frame.

His last night, Wednesday the 12th, he was pulling at pants, asking for hugs. He got lots of hugs and lots of kisses. His breath was bad — a bath was planned for the morning. He was carried to bed because he wouldn't come by himself — he knew about the stained sheets. He got his morning kisses, same as always.

Thursday morning, they woke up together. Morning kisses. He didn't seem so sick that anything was seriously wrong. His owner took a shower, and when he came out — Jett was gone. Normally he'd wait for breakfast. He never came.

He didn't go outside. He went back inside and hid between boxes in a corner. He likely passed within hours. He never made a sound — no cry, no call for help, nothing. His owner called his name, shouted for him. He was just a few meters away.

Found seven days later, on March 19th, by chance. A neighbor came by. They found him together.
